Solutions & Prevention for Norovirus

Treatment & Management

Norovirus usually resolves on its own, but proper care can speed up recovery:

  • Rehydration: Use ORS, electrolyte water, coconut water, or broth.
  • Diet Adjustments: Follow the BRAT diet. Avoid heavy, spicy, or oily food.
  • Medications:
    • 💊 Anti-nausea: Ondansetron in severe vomiting.
    • 🤒 Pain relief: Paracetamol to reduce fever and pain.
    • 🚫 No anti-diarrheal drugs without doctor advice.

Prevention Tips

  • 🧼 Handwashing: Wash with soap; sanitizer isn’t enough!
  • 🍱 Safe Food Practices: Clean fruits, cook seafood, avoid food from sick people.
  • 🧽 Disinfect: Use bleach-based cleaners.
  • 🏠 Isolation: Avoid contact for 48 hours after symptoms stop.
  • 🧺 Laundry: Wash soiled clothes in hot water and dry fully.

Do's & Don'ts

✅ Do's

  • Drink fluids and ORS frequently.
  • Disinfect surfaces regularly.
  • Wash hands for 20+ seconds.
  • Rest and isolate.

❌ Don'ts

  • Avoid sugary or fizzy drinks.
  • Don’t skip meals entirely.
  • Don't ignore persistent vomiting.
  • No food prep while infected.

Norovirus vs. Other Bugs

  • Food Poisoning: May include fever & longer recovery.
  • Rotavirus: Affects kids mostly; vaccine available.
  • Flu: Respiratory issues—not usually gut-related.

Remember: Norovirus spreads fast but is manageable with care. Hydration and hygiene are your best defense!
