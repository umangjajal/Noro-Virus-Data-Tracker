Symptoms of Norovirus

How Norovirus Spreads

  • Person-to-Person Contact: Shaking hands, sharing utensils, or caring for an infected person.
  • Contaminated Food & Water: Eating food prepared by an infected person or drinking contaminated water.
  • Surface Contamination: Touching infected surfaces (doorknobs, tables, shared electronics) and then touching the mouth.
  • Aerosolized Vomit Particles: Tiny particles can become airborne when an infected person vomits.

High-Risk Environments

  • Hospitals & Nursing Homes: Elderly and immunocompromised individuals are at higher risk.
  • Schools & Daycare Centers: Close contact and poor hygiene lead to rapid spread.
  • Cruise Ships & Restaurants: Outbreaks occur due to shared food and water sources.

Incubation & Duration

  • Symptoms appear 12 to 48 hours after exposure.
  • The illness lasts 1 to 3 days, but shedding of the virus can continue for up to 2 weeks.

Complications of Norovirus

While norovirus usually resolves on its own, severe cases may lead to:

  • Severe Dehydration: May require hospitalization, especially for young children and elderly individuals.
  • Electrolyte Imbalance: Loss of essential minerals can cause dizziness and irregular heartbeat.
  • Malnutrition: Persistent vomiting and diarrhea can lead to nutrient deficiencies.
  • Secondary Infections: Weakened immunity may make individuals vulnerable to other infections.

Diagnosis & Testing

  • Clinical Diagnosis: Based on symptoms, especially during outbreaks.
  • Laboratory Testing: Stool samples can confirm norovirus in severe cases.
